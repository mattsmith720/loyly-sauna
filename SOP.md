## 0. Golden rules (read first, every shift)

1. **Timber is a living, porous, unsealed surface.** Treat cedar/pine benches and walls like a fine musical instrument, not a bathroom floor.
2. **Never introduce standing or running water into a sauna.** Damp microfibre only. Moisture rots bench undersides and framing and voids the asset we are paid to protect.
3. **No bleach. No ammonia. No pressure washer. Ever.** No exceptions, no "just this once." See Section 1.
4. **The heater is electrical and can exceed 400°C in use.** Never touch, wet or clean a hot heater. Confirm isolation before you go near it. See Section 7.
5. **If in doubt, stop and photograph it.** A photo and a text to the operator beats a guess. We never improvise chemistry or method on a client asset.
6. **Every visit produces a before-and-after photo set.** No photos = the visit did not happen and does not get invoiced. See Section 6.
7. **A signed pre-service condition report exists for every site before we ever touch it.** Pre-existing damage is documented so we are never blamed for it.

## 1. Timber-safe chemistry rules

### 1.1 The hard DO-NOTs (and exactly why)

| Never use | Why it destroys the asset / creates liability |
|---|---|
| **Chlorine bleach (sodium hypochlorite)** | Soaks into porous timber and leaves residue. On next heat-up (80 - 100°C) the residue re-volatilises, releasing chlorine gas into a small sealed room full of people. Also bleaches and greys the cedar and corrodes heater elements and fasteners. |
| **Ammonia / ammonia-based glass or multi-surface sprays** | Same re-volatilisation risk under heat, respiratory hazard in an enclosed hot room, and reacts dangerously if it ever contacts chlorine residue. Strips timber oils. |
| **Pressure washer** | Drives water deep into grain and joints, delaminates and furs the timber surface, and floods framing and bench undersides that never dry, causing rot and mould we then get blamed for. Instant asset damage. |
| **Running water / hosing / bucket-sloshing / wet-mopping a saturated mop** | Timber is unsealed. Standing water swells the boards, lifts fibres, warps benches, rots undersides, and rusts fasteners. Saunas are cleaned essentially dry. |
| **Vinegar or acidic descalers on timber** | Acids etch and grey raw cedar/pine and strip protective oils. (Diluted vinegar is acceptable on **glass and stainless only**, see 1.3, never on wood.) |
| **Oil-soap / wax-build products (e.g. Murphy-style oil soaps), furniture polish, silicone sprays** | Leave a film that goes rancid and smokes under heat, traps grime, and makes the bench slippery and sticky. |
| **Fragrance/essential oils applied to timber or poured neat on stones** | Neat oils on hot stones flash and can ignite; oils on timber oxidise and smell burnt. Aroma is the venue's call via their own löyly water, not a cleaning product. |
| **Coloured/dyed or film-forming "deck" sealers, polyurethane, varnish** | Sealing a sauna bench traps heat against skin (burns), blisters, and peels. Sauna timber is only ever oiled/waxed with breathable heat-rated product, never film-sealed. |
| **Magic-eraser melamine pads on timber** | Abrade and lighten the surface unevenly; leave scuff-white patches on cedar. |

**One-line version for the cleaner:** *If it foams, bleaches, smells like a pool or a cleaning cupboard, or comes out of a pressure hose, it does not go near sauna timber.*

### 1.2 Approved product list (timber-safe, sourced in Australia)

Keep a current **SDS (Safety Data Sheet) for every product in the kit**, in the SDS folder in the vehicle and a copy in ServiceM8. Buy small, buy known brands, log the batch.

**A. Wood-safe sauna cleaner (daily/maintenance surface clean)**
- **Type:** pH-neutral, low-residue, biodegradable timber/sauna cleaner. Non-film-forming, no bleach/ammonia/solvent smell.
- **Named examples available in AU:**
  - **Tikkurila Supi Saunapesu** (Finnish sauna cleaner, sold by AU timber-finish and sauna suppliers) - the category benchmark, dilutes in warm water, formulated exactly for sauna wood.
  - **Harvia sauna cleaning agent** (imported with Harvia heaters/accessories via AU sauna retailers).
  - **Eucalyptus-oil-free, pH-neutral timber wash** as a backup: **Bio-Glo / Enviro-branded neutral timber cleaners** or a **pH-neutral Original-type sugar-soap-free neutral cleaner** heavily diluted - only if a dedicated sauna product is unavailable, and only pH-neutral.
- **Source in AU:** dedicated sauna retailers (Sauna HQ, Aussie Health Co, Nordic/Scandinavian sauna importers), Tikkurila stockists, and specialist timber-finish suppliers. Order 2 - 3 bottles at a time; it lasts.

**B. Paraffin sauna wax / sauna bench oil (protective treatment - quarterly/annual)**
- **Type:** heat-resistant paraffin-based sauna wax or natural sauna bench oil, breathable, food-safe/skin-safe when cured, formulated for the hot bench-contact zone.
- **Named examples available in AU:**
  - **Supi Saunavaha (Tikkurila)** - paraffin-based sauna wax, the specified product for benches, backrests and door handles. Available clear and lightly tinted.
  - **Supi Saunasuoja / Supi Laudesuoja** - sauna protectant / bench oil for walls and benches respectively.
  - **Harvia SmartFold / Harvia sauna wood oil** and **Sauna-branded paraffin bench oils** from AU sauna importers as alternatives.
  - For **walls/ceiling** (non-contact): **Supi Saunasuoja** rather than wax.
- **Source in AU:** Tikkurila stockists, specialist sauna retailers, high-end timber-finish suppliers. Keep clear (non-tinting) as default so we never change the client's timber colour without sign-off.

**C. Antimicrobial timber-safe treatment (mould/bacteria control - quarterly, and spot use)**
- **Type:** timber-safe, no-bleach sanitiser/mould treatment rated for food-contact/porous surfaces; leaves no volatile residue that re-gasses under heat. Prefer **hydrogen-peroxide-based** or **quaternary/food-grade sanitiser** or **plant-based (thymol) botanical disinfectant**.
  - **Hydrogen peroxide 3% (pharmacy grade)** - the workhorse for spot mould on timber and for wiping benches; breaks down to water and oxygen, leaves no re-gassing residue. Apply damp, dwell, wipe, let air-dry.
  - **Botanical/thymol disinfectants - e.g. Thymox / Benefect Botanical Disinfectant** (thyme-oil based, food-contact rated, timber-friendly) for antimicrobial passes.
  - **Isopropyl alcohol 70%** for hard non-porous fittings (handles, hinges, thermometer housings), not for flooding timber.
  - **Oxygen bleach (sodium percarbonate)** - diluted, damp-applied, **only** where a client insists on stain lifting; rinse-free formulations preferred and always wiped dry. **This is not chlorine bleach.** Never confuse the two.
- **Source in AU:** pharmacy (H2O2, IPA), janitorial suppliers (Thymox/Benefect, oxygen bleach), grocery for food-grade options.

**D. Glass, stainless & fittings**
- **Type:** streak-free glass cleaner **ammonia-free** (e.g. an **ammonia-free glass cleaner**), or **diluted white vinegar solution used only on glass/stainless**, plus microfibre. IPA 70% for handles and touchpoints.

**E. Heater / stones**
- Stones are cleaned **dry** (brush + vacuum) or rinsed **outside the sauna** in plain water and dried before returning. No chemical on sauna stones ever.

> **Product change control:** No product enters the kit without (a) an SDS on file, (b) confirmation it is pH-neutral or peroxide/botanical based, non-film-forming, no chlorine/ammonia, and (c) owner sign-off. The approved list above is the whole list. Adding to it is an owner decision, logged in this SOP.

### 1.3 Dilution and residue rule

- Follow the **product label dilution** for sauna cleaner; when unlabelled, err weaker.
- Everything applied to timber is applied to a **damp (wrung-out) microfibre**, never sprayed to saturation and never left wet.
- **Wipe on, wipe off, leave the timber to air-dry with the door open and vent open.** No product film is left behind on a bench that a person will sit on at 90°C.

## 3. Maintenance clean procedure (~45 min per sauna)

**Frequency:** weekly or fortnightly per contract. **Goal:** hygienic, dry, salt-free, mould-free, guest-ready, fully photographed. **Recovery studios prefer 5 - 8am pre-open slots.**

**Time budget totals 45 minutes.** Adjust proportionally for very small (barrel/infrared, ~30 min) or large (multi-tier commercial, ~55 - 60 min) rooms and price accordingly.

> **STEP 0 - Arrive & isolate (3 min).** Confirm the heater is **OFF and cold** (IR thermometer on stones/element: must read near ambient). If the venue leaves it on a timer, have the manager switch it off and, for deep work, isolate at the wall. Open the door fully and open the vent. Lay drop sheet at the threshold. **Take all BEFORE photos now** (Section 6) before disturbing anything.

1. **Dry debris removal - floors, under-bench, heater surround (6 min).** HEPA-vacuum the floor, then **under and behind every bench** (this is where hair, skin, dust and mould food collect and where generic cleaners never reach), then the heater surround and skirting. Crevice tool into all corners and the duckboard/slatted floor gaps. Nothing wet yet.

2. **Ceiling and upper walls, top-down (5 min).** Working top-down so debris falls to the not-yet-cleaned floor zone: dust the ceiling and upper wall boards with a dry then barely-damp **blue** microfibre. Light wipe only; timber up high rarely needs product.

3. **Timber walls & backrests wipe (6 min).** Damp (wrung-out) **blue** microfibre with wood-safe sauna cleaner at label dilution. Wipe wall boards and backrests along the grain. Re-wring often. Surfaces should come up clean and **only just damp**, never wet.

4. **Sweat-salt soft-brush pass on benches (7 min).** Benches carry white **sweat-salt and body-oil deposits** that a flat wipe smears rather than lifts. Lightly mist the bench with sauna cleaner solution (or work off a damp cloth), then work a **soft natural-bristle brush** along the grain in short strokes to lift salt and oil out of the pores. Wipe residue away with a clean damp blue cloth. Turn attention to the **front lip and seat edge** (highest skin contact). Follow with a light antimicrobial wipe (H2O2 3% or botanical) on the seating surface; let it dwell 1 - 2 min. **This step is the visible difference between us and a generic cleaner and belongs in the after-photos.**

5. **UV-torch mould & residue check (4 min).** Dim/turn off the sauna light. Sweep the **UV torch** across **bench undersides, the back corners, wall/bench junctions, behind backrests, and the base of the heater surround** - the low-airflow, damp-trapping zones. Organic residue and early mould fluoresce. **Photograph anything that lights up.** Spot-treat live mould with H2O2 3% or botanical disinfectant, dwell, wipe, and note it in the report. Anything beyond spot-treatment (spreading mould, black staining into grain) is flagged for a deep clean/restoration, not forced in the 45-minute window.

6. **Heater & stones quick check (3 min).** With stones **cold**: HEPA-vacuum dust and debris off the stone bed and heater guard. Visually check for cracked/crumbling stones and note (do not replace during a maintenance clean unless contracted). No chemical, no water on stones.

7. **Glass, door, tracks, lights, vents, thermometer (5 min).** **Green** cloth + ammonia-free glass cleaner on the glass door/panel (both sides). Detail-brush and wipe the **door tracks and hinges** (grit and hair collect here). Wipe the **light housing, thermometer/hygrometer, timer, and vent louvres**; detail-brush the vent slots (airflow is hygiene). IPA 70% on the **door handle and high-touch points**.

8. **Floor finish & duckboard (4 min).** Lift duckboards/slatted mats if present, vacuum beneath, wipe the substrate floor and drain grate with a **red** damp cloth and neutral cleaner, and check the drain runs free. Return duckboards dry. For tiled/stone floors under the timber, damp-wipe - never flood.

9. **Reset, air-out, final check (2 min).** Return benches/mats/headrests to spec, confirm nothing is left damp, leave the **door open and vent open to air-dry** (agree with the venue how long before they re-heat). Remove drop sheet and all waste.

> **STEP 10 - AFTER photos + report (built into the above / on exit).** Capture the full after-photo set (Section 6). ServiceM8 auto-sends the before/after report to the venue manager. Log any flags (mould, cracked stones, loose fastener, drainage) as line items for the next deep clean.

## 4. Quarterly deep clean / restoration procedure (2 - 4 hrs)

**Frequency:** quarterly per commercial contract; wax/oil at minimum annually; one-off for residential. **Goal:** restore raw timber, re-protect it, sanitise deep, service the heater/stones and hardware, and issue a condition report that defers the venue's $20 - 60k refurbishment.

> **STEP 0 - Isolate, protect, assess, photograph (15 min).**
> - **Confirm heater cold, then isolate the heater circuit at the wall/isolator** (deep work near the heater and dust demands the circuit dead, not just off). Verify with IR thermometer and, where fitted, the isolation switch. Never rely on a timer.
> - Full BEFORE photo set + close-ups of every defect (Section 6). Take **moisture-meter readings** at benches, bench undersides and lower walls; log them.
> - Mask/protect the heater, light, thermometer and vents from sanding dust. Drop sheets down. Set up **dust extraction connected to the sander.**

1. **Full maintenance clean first (20 min).** Run Section 3 steps 1 - 8 so you are sanding a clean, salt-free, dry surface. Sanding dirty salted timber just drives grime in.

2. **Dustless orbital sanding of benches & contact surfaces (45 - 70 min).** This is the signature "raw cedar restored" service.
   - Sander **dust port connected to the extractor at all times** (dustless method - no airborne cedar dust, clean site, respiratory safety, and it is the sellable before/after).
   - Start at **120 grit** to cut through greyed/oxidised surface and stains, progress **150 → 180 → 240** for a smooth, skin-friendly finish. Do not skip grits.
   - Sand **with the grain**, keep the sander moving, light even pressure, never dwell (dwelling gouges and burns).
   - **Detail-sand** edges, front lips, backrests, corners and slats by hand block / detail sander.
   - Prioritise **seat and back contact surfaces**; walls and ceiling are sanded only if greyed or stained (usually a lighter pass or skip).
   - Vacuum the whole room with HEPA, then tack-wipe with a **just-damp** cloth to lift residual dust. Let flash-dry.

3. **Antimicrobial treatment (15 min).** Apply timber-safe antimicrobial (H2O2 3% or botanical disinfectant) across benches, undersides, wall/bench junctions and corners - especially any zone that fluoresced under UV. Dwell per product, wipe, and **UV-recheck** to confirm the mould/residue is gone. Photograph the UV before/after. Note any mould that has penetrated the grain beyond restoration as a defect in the report.

4. **Oil / wax protective treatment (30 - 45 min including cure windows).** Timber must be **clean, dry and cool.**
   - **Benches, backrests, front lips, door handle:** **Supi Saunavaha** paraffin sauna wax (or approved), thin even coat with a **yellow** lint-free cloth/foam pad, **with the grain.** Wipe back any excess - a thin cured film, never a sticky build.
   - **Walls / ceiling (non-contact):** **Supi Saunasuoja** protectant rather than wax where specified.
   - Respect the product's **cure/recoat time** before a second thin coat if required. Default to **clear/untinted** product so timber colour is unchanged; any tint is a signed client decision.
   - **Advise the venue not to heat the sauna for the product's stated cure period** (record the time; typically several hours to overnight). Note it in the report and confirm with the manager.

5. **Heater-stone inspection / replacement (15 - 25 min).**
   - With stones cold and circuit isolated, **remove all stones.** Inspect each: discard any **cracked, crumbled, flaking or fused** stones (they choke airflow and can damage the element). Vacuum the element and heater basket of accumulated stone dust and debris.
   - **Rinse reusable stones in plain water outside the sauna, dry them,** and **re-stack loosely** (airflow gaps, larger stones at the bottom, element not smothered) per the heater manufacturer's pattern.
   - **Replace to the correct fill level** with approved sauna stones (peridotite/olivine/vulcanite) of the right size. **High-use commercial heaters: full stone replacement ~annually** - schedule it. Log stone condition and replacement in the report; stone replacement labour+supply is a chargeable line item.

6. **Fastener check & tighten (10 min).** Heat and humidity cycles **loosen screws and bolts.** Systematically check and tighten **bench brackets, backrest fixings, wall-board screws, bench-support framing, door hinges, handle, and duckboard fasteners.** Do not overtighten into soft/heat-softened timber. Note any stripped, rusted or missing fasteners or any bench with movement as a defect and safety item.

7. **Ventilation & drainage check (10 min).** Clear and vacuum **intake (low, near heater) and exhaust (high) vents**; confirm louvres move and airflow is unobstructed (this is the primary mould-prevention control). Check the **floor drain runs free**, flush lightly (a controlled small amount at the drain only, never over timber), and confirm no pooling under duckboards. Note any blocked vent or slow drain.

8. **Hardware, glass, light, controls (10 min).** Detail glass, door seal/gasket, hinges, thermometer/hygrometer accuracy sanity-check, light and control panel. Confirm the door closes and seals correctly and the bench is solid to sit on.

9. **Condition report + refurb-deferral notes (15 min).** Complete the written **Condition Report** (Section 4.1) in ServiceM8 with all photos, moisture readings, UV findings, stone status, fastener/vent/drain notes, work performed, products used (with batch), cure/re-heat instructions, and a plain-English **"refurbishment deferral" summary**: what we preserved, what to watch, and the recommended next action. This is the document that justifies our fee against a $20 - 60k refurb and pre-empts a damage dispute.

> **STEP 10 - De-isolate & handover.** Restore power to the heater **only after** confirming stones are correctly stacked, no tools/rags remain, and the venue understands the no-heat cure window. AFTER photo set complete. Remove all dust, drop sheets and **oily rags into the sealed disposal tub** (Section 7).

### 4.1 Condition report - required fields
Site & sauna ID · Date/time · Cleaner · Timber type & approx. age · Moisture readings (bench top / bench underside / lower wall) · UV mould findings (before/after) · Sanding grits used & surfaces done · Product + batch (cleaner / wax / antimicrobial) · Cure / no-reheat window given to venue · Stone condition & count replaced · Fasteners tightened / defects · Vent & drain status · Glass/hardware · **Refurb-deferral summary & recommended next visit** · Full before/after photo set · Client pre-existing-damage acknowledgement reference.

## 5. Ice bath / cold plunge - surface & water-hygiene add-on ($60 - 90/visit)

**Scope:** the tub surface and immediate hygiene. **We are not the plant/pump technician** and we do not dose the venue's chemistry beyond what is contracted and labelled; where a venue runs filtration/ozone/UV, we service the surfaces and confirm the system is running, and flag faults.

> **STEP 0 - Confirm status & photograph.** Confirm whether we are draining/refilling or surface-servicing a running system this visit (per contract). BEFORE photos of water clarity, waterline, interior and surrounds. Note water temp and, if the venue has test strips/meter, log **pH, sanitiser (chlorine/bromine or mineral), and clarity.**

1. **PPE & isolate.** Nitrile gloves, eye protection. If draining, confirm pump/heater/chiller is switched off before draining to avoid dry-running the equipment.
2. **Surrounds & steps first.** Clean and sanitise the **steps, lid, edges and grab rails** (highest contact, biofilm risk) with an appropriate surface sanitiser for the tub material (stainless, acrylic, poly, or timber-clad - match the product to the material; timber cladding follows Section 1 rules).
3. **Waterline & interior (drained service).** Drain per site method. **Match cleaner to tub material:**
   - **Stainless / acrylic / poly tubs:** non-abrasive tub cleaner or diluted white-vinegar solution on the waterline scum ring, soft cloth/non-scratch pad, then rinse and wipe. No bleach if the tub is timber-clad or timber-lined.
   - **Timber (ofuro-style) tubs:** treat the timber to Section 1 rules - no bleach, no soak, wood-safe method only.
   - Address **biofilm at the waterline and in fittings** - this is the actual hygiene risk in cold plunges (people, not heat, so bacteria thrive).
4. **Sanitise interior.** Wipe down with a material-appropriate sanitiser; for non-timber tubs a food-grade/peroxide or approved sanitiser, dwell, rinse.
5. **Filter & fittings (if contracted / accessible).** Rinse or swap the cartridge filter, clear skimmer/hair-lint basket, wipe jets and returns. Log filter condition.
6. **Refill & confirm.** Refill to level, restart pump/chiller, and confirm circulation. Where contracted, **balance and log chemistry** to the venue's target (pH ~7.2 - 7.6 and sanitiser per their standard) using their dosing products, or confirm the automated system's readout. Never mix dosing chemicals; add to water, never water to chemical.
7. **Cold plunge specifics.** Confirm **chiller is running and hitting target temp**; note actual temp. Check lid seal and insulation. Flag any cloudiness, odour, biofilm or equipment fault immediately to the manager (a green/cloudy plunge is a Google-review and health risk we are paid to prevent).

> **STEP 8 - AFTER photos + log.** Clear-water shot, clean waterline, steps/surrounds, and chemistry log. Feed into the venue report alongside the sauna.

## 7. Safety

### 7.1 PPE (minimum)
- **P2 respirator** whenever sanding, vacuuming fine dust, or using disinfectants in an enclosed room. Cedar/pine dust is a **respiratory sensitiser** - sanding without a mask and extraction is prohibited.
- **Safety glasses** for sanding, overhead work and chemical spray.
- **Hearing protection** while sanding.
- **Nitrile gloves** for all chemical and ice-bath work.
- **Knee pads** for under-bench and floor work.
- Enclosed non-slip footwear. Tie back hair. No loose sleeves near the sander.

### 7.2 Dust extraction (mandatory on sand)
- The orbital sander is **always connected to dust extraction** ("dustless" is a method rule, not a marketing line). No extraction = do not sand.
- Ventilate: door open, vent open, during and after sanding.
- HEPA-vacuum, do not sweep dry, to avoid re-aerosolising fine dust.
- **Oily/waxy rags are a spontaneous-combustion hazard.** Paraffin/oil-soaked cloths can self-heat and ignite. Store used oil/wax rags in a **sealed metal or lidded non-combustible tub, submerged in water**, and dispose per waste rules. Never leave them balled up in a bag in the van or bin.

### 7.3 Electrical / heater safety
- **Assume the heater is live and hot until proven otherwise.** Confirm cold with the IR thermometer (stones/element at ~ambient) and OFF at the control before any work near it.
- For **deep cleans and any stone handling, isolate the heater circuit at the wall isolator/switchboard** and verify. Restore power only at handover after stones are correctly restacked.
- **Never** apply water or chemical to a heater or hot stones. Never clean a heater that has been on within the last few hours - it holds heat.
- Keep water and damp cloths away from the heater, light fittings, thermometer wiring and control panel. Report any exposed wiring, scorched fitting, tripping circuit or damaged element to the venue and owner immediately; do not attempt electrical repair (not our scope).
- Correct **stone stacking** matters for safety: loose, airflow-permitting, element never smothered, correct fill level and stone type per the heater manufacturer. A packed heater overheats.

### 7.4 Working around timber + heat + confined space
- A sauna is a **small, enclosed, hot-capable room** - treat it as a confined workspace. Prop the door open; never let it latch you in. Have the venue know you are in there.
- If the room is still hot from use, **wait for it to cool** or have it turned off well ahead; do not work in a >40°C room.
- Watch for **slippery benches** after oil/wax and wet floors - signage/verbal warning to venue staff.
- **Manual handling:** benches and duckboards are heavy and awkward; lift with legs, get help for large tiers, watch fingers in bracket gaps.
- **Chemical safety:** never mix products; keep the SDS folder in the vehicle and on ServiceM8; store products labelled and upright; know the nearest tap/eyewash. If any product ever contacts a hot surface and smokes, ventilate and evacuate the room.
- **Slips/trips:** cables from sander/vac and drop sheets are trip hazards - route tidily.

### 7.5 Damage-liability discipline (protects the business)
- **No site is worked without a signed pre-service condition report** documenting existing damage, and public-liability certificate of currency on file with the venue.
- Photograph pre-existing damage on arrival, every visit.
- Any incident (scratch, drop, crack, water spill, tripped circuit) is photographed and reported to the owner and venue the same day. Honesty and documentation, always.
