## 1. What this is / the bet / the ceiling

LOYLY is a cash-flow operator asset, not a startup. The bet: nobody in Brisbane owns recurring specialist sauna hygiene, "sauna cleaning Brisbane" has near-zero competition, and a documented timber-safe method plus a photo-audit report is a moat a general cleaner cannot copy. You are selling proof and risk removal (defer a $20-60k refurb, stay audit-ready) at trade margins ($80-150/hr effective, ~60% gross), not cleaning by the hour. Realistic single-city ceiling is ~$200-400k revenue; base case is ~$150k ARR / ~$75-95k owner profit on 15 sites in 12 months, run on under 4 hrs/week of your time by month 4. The only two levers that decide $72k vs $270k are CAC (how cheaply you land the next contract) and route density (how tightly it clusters into an existing cleaner-day). Do not confuse activity with either.

## 7. RISKS TO WATCH (and the mitigation already built into the assets)

- **You damage a client's $30-80k asset → uninsured claim.** Mitigation built in: signed pre-service Condition Report on every site before you touch it (Sales Pack §4), PL $10-20M with care-custody-control, SDS folder, "assume heater live and hot" isolation rule (SOP §7). No site worked without report + certificate.
- **A cleaner uses the wrong product and rots the timber.** Mitigation: SOP §1 hard DO-NOTs + §8 laminated Quick Card + the "if it foams/bleaches/smells like a pool it doesn't touch timber" rule. Product change control is an owner-only decision.
- **Key-person cleaner quits and breaks the weekly rounds.** Mitigation: the SOP is written so a new hire is solo in a week (3-site training path, Ops Doc §3); cross-train a 2nd casual by month 4.
- **Scattered sign-ups kill margin.** Mitigation: cluster-by-day zoning + nearest-neighbour selling + the >15-site expansion gate (Ops Doc §4). Sell density into every close.
- **Underpricing / margin leak on wages.** Mitigation: every pay/super/loading figure is a hard VERIFY gate against the live Fair Work Cleaning Services Award before you quote a margin or sign the casual (Ops Doc §3, §5). Early-morning (5-8am) penalty rates must be recovered in the Premium slot loading.
- **A visit gets disputed or an invoice questioned.** Mitigation: no-photo = no-invoice (SOP §6), and the photo-report guarantee ("if a visit isn't documented, that visit is free") is contractual (Sales Pack §3, §7). The matched before/after set is both the product and the defence.
- **CAC creeps up chasing cold leads.** Mitigation: prioritise the two near-zero-CAC channels you already own (SPARR gym pipeline §4a, Blake/SolarShield lead swap + RepFlow flywheel §4b) before spending on Ads. One warm intro beats ten cold emails.
