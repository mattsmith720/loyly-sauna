# LOYLY CO. - Ops Setup & Unit Economics

Pure-play specialist sauna cleaning, hygiene and restoration. Brisbane first, then SEQ. Operator-run, one casual cleaner, documented SOP, recurring commercial contracts. Target $120-180k ARR in 12 months at 50-65% gross margin, running on under 4 hrs/week of Matt's time by month 4.

> Pricing throughout is ex-GST unless stated. All pay, super and insurance figures must be confirmed against the live source (Fair Work pay guide, insurer quote) before you commit spend or sign a worker. Where you see "VERIFY", treat it as a hard gate, not a footnote.

## 2. Admin Setup Checklist

Work top to bottom. Items marked (GATE) block you from sending a proposal or booking a paid job.

- [ ] **ABN + business name** - Register at **business.gov.au** (single flow for ABN + business name via ASIC). Business name "LOYLY CO." ~$44 (1 yr) / ~$102 (3 yr). ABN is free. Structure: start as sole trader unless you want the company; you already have SPARR GLOBAL PTY LTD as a template if you decide to run LOYLY through a Pty Ltd later. **Decision to log:** sole trader vs new Pty Ltd (liability on expensive timber/heaters argues for a company + insurance).
- [ ] **GST registration (GATE for pricing)** - Register for GST via the ABN application or ATO Business Portal. You are pricing ex-GST and quoting commercial venues who claim it back, so register from day 1. All quotes and invoices show GST separately.
- [ ] **Business bank account** - Separate transaction account (keep clean books; do not mix with SPARR/RepFlow). CommBank/Up/Tyro. Note: SPARR already has a CommBank ID-verification blocker on file, so allow lead time.
- [ ] **Public liability insurance $10-20M (GATE - certificate goes on EVERY proposal)** - Get quotes from **BizCover**, **Coverforce**, **Aon**, or **Gallagher** for a cleaning/property-services risk with a timber-restoration and power-tool component. Specify: work on high-value timber assets ($30-80k saunas), electric heaters, sanding. Ask for tool cover and a "care, custody and control" extension (you are working ON the client's expensive asset). Get a **Certificate of Currency** PDF ready to attach to proposals. Budget $1,000-1,500/yr.
- [ ] **Xero + chart of accounts** - Set up Xero; connect the bank feed; GST/BAS coding on. Categories: maintenance revenue, deep-clean revenue, add-on revenue, product-supply revenue; COGS = labour, consumables, fuel. Set BAS quarterly.
- [ ] **Stripe invoicing** - Connect Stripe to Xero (or use Stripe payment links inside Xero invoices). Set up: recurring invoice schedules for monthly contracts, saved card / autopay for commercial clients, 7-day terms. This is the "runs itself" engine for recurring contracts.
- [ ] **ServiceM8 setup (GATE for operator-run)** - This is the ops spine. Configure:
  - **Job templates:** (1) Maintenance Clean ~45 min/sauna, (2) Quarterly Deep Clean/Restoration 2-4 hr, (3) Ice-bath/Plunge add-on, (4) Steam-room deep clean, (5) Stone replacement, (6) Residential one-off deep clean.
  - **Mandatory photo checklist workflow (the moat):** each job type has a required checklist the cleaner cannot close without: BEFORE photos (each bench, under-bench, heater surround, floor, glass/door, vents), method steps ticked (dry vac → wood-safe wipe → soft-brush sweat-salt → glass/tracks/vents → UV mould spot-check), AFTER photos matching the before angles, and a notes field for condition/refurb-deferral flags. Make photos a required field so no visit closes without proof.
  - **Auto-report to venue manager:** configure the job-completion PDF/email to auto-send the before/after report to the site contact.
  - **Recurring schedule:** weekly/fortnightly recurring jobs per contract, clustered by suburb/day (see section 4).
  - **Monthly spot-audit template:** a Matt-only QA job type for the monthly audit.
- [ ] **Google Business Profile (GATE for SEO)** - Create/verify GBP for LOYLY CO., category "Commercial cleaning service" / "Cleaning service". Service area = Brisbane + SEQ (service-area business, no public storefront). Load before/after photos, service list, and start collecting reviews (RepFlow flywheel) from job one. This plus a landing page is how you own "sauna cleaning Brisbane".
- [ ] **SDS chemical folder (GATE - compliance + sales asset)** - Build a folder (physical in vehicle + digital in Drive) with the Safety Data Sheet for every product, plus a one-page "Timber-Safe Product List" stating you never use bleach/ammonia/pressure washers. Attach the timber-safe list to proposals; carry SDS in the vehicle for any site audit.
- [ ] **Signed pre-service condition report (GATE - damage-liability shield)** - Template a per-site condition report (photos + timber/heater condition at first visit, signed by venue). This plus insurance is your defence on any "you damaged our sauna" claim. Store in ServiceM8 job attachments.
- [ ] **WorkCover QLD - set up ONLY when you hire (GATE before the cleaner's first paid shift)** - Register for a WorkCover Queensland Accident Insurance policy at **worksafe.qld.gov.au** before the casual does any paid work. Premium is a % of wages by industry classification; declare wages annually. Do not let the cleaner start unregistered.
- [ ] **Superannuation setup** - Nominate a default fund / set up SuperStream via Xero for the casual (12% super, VERIFY current SG rate). Pay via Xero auto-super.

## 3. Hiring Plan - One Casual Cleaner

You do the first ~10 jobs yourself to build and prove the SOP, then hire one reliable casual who is comfortable with power tools and cleared to work.

### Where to advertise
- **SEEK** and **Indeed** - "Specialist sauna cleaner / restoration technician (casual)". Frame as trade-adjacent, not commodity cleaning, to attract the right person.
- **Facebook local jobs + Gumtree** - Brisbane trades/cleaning groups.
- **SPARR + gym network** - combat gyms and recovery studios know reliable, fit, early-rising people; ask partners for referrals.
- **Blake/SolarShield (GUNNR) network** - same labour pool (soft-wash/pressure-wash crews); ask for a spare reliable casual or a referral.
- **Backpacker / working-holiday boards** - good for pre-open early shifts, but weigh against continuity (you want low churn given the SOP investment).

### Scorecard (traits to score 1-5, hire only 4+ average with no 1s on non-negotiables)
- **Reliability / punctuality (non-negotiable)** - 5-8am pre-open slots mean lateness = angry venue.
- **Comfortable with power tools** - orbital sander, driver; not intimidated by the restoration work.
- **Care and hands** - gentle, methodical; understands the asset is worth $30-80k and the point is to protect it.
- **Photo/process discipline** - will actually complete the ServiceM8 photo checklist every time.
- **Presentable + trustworthy** - solo, unsupervised, inside client premises before open. **Police check mandatory** (non-negotiable).
- **Physical fit** - early starts, kneeling/under-bench work, some heat.
- **Low-drama / low-churn signal** - you are investing a week of training; you want them for 12+ months.

### Training path - 3 supervised sites
1. **Site 1 (observe + assist):** Matt runs the full SOP; cleaner shadows, does the vac/brush steps, watches the sanding and the photo workflow. Debrief against the checklist.
2. **Site 2 (lead with supervision):** Cleaner runs the maintenance clean end to end; Matt supervises, corrects, and signs off the photo report. Introduce UV mould check and fastener check.
3. **Site 3 (solo with spot-check):** Cleaner runs solo; Matt arrives for the final 15 min to audit before/after photos and condition notes. Sign off as "cleared solo" only if the checklist is clean.
- Target: **productive/solo within one week** (this is the whole point of documenting the SOP to a licensable standard). Deep-clean/sanding gets its own supervised session before they do it unsupervised.

### Pay - Cleaning Services Award (VERIFY before hiring)
- Award: **Cleaning Services Award 2020 (MA000022)**. Pull the **current Fair Work Ombudsman pay guide** (fairwork.gov.au) for the exact base rate at the correct classification level on the day you hire. Do not rely on last year's number.
- Build the fully-loaded casual rate as: **award base + 25% casual loading + 12% superannuation** (VERIFY both the loading and the SG rate are current), plus penalty rates for early/weekend hours where they apply. The 5-8am pre-open slots may attract early-morning penalty loadings under the award - check and price them into the premium slot.
- Working figure used in the economics below: **~$33-36/hr fully loaded**. Reconfirm against the live pay guide before the first shift and before quoting margins to anyone.
- Keep a wage record; declare wages to WorkCover QLD and run super via Xero SuperStream.

### Backup / key-person risk (cross-train by month 4)
- **Key-person cleaner is a named risk.** By month 4, cross-train a **second casual** (or a reliable SolarShield/SPARR-network person) through the same 3-site path so a single sickness/quit does not break contracted weekly rounds.
- The SOP doc is the insurance: written to the level a new hire is productive in a week means the backup is cheap to spin up (and it is what makes the whole thing licensable later).

## 4. Scheduling & Route-Density Playbook

Route density, not price or technique, decides $80k vs $200k+. The scheduling rules exist to protect margin per cleaner-day.

- **Cluster by suburb/day.** Assign geographic zones to fixed days so a full day is spent in one pocket, not crossing the city. Illustrative BNE zoning:
  - **Mon** - Inner-north (Red Hill / Newstead / Fortitude Valley) - Vikasati Red Hill, valley studios.
  - **Tue** - Inner-south + West End / South Brisbane - bathhouses, gyms.
  - **Wed** - Eastern suburbs / Bayside.
  - **Thu** - Northside corridor (Chermside / North Lakes).
  - **Fri** - CBD hotels + apartment communal saunas + quarterly deep-clean block.
  - Deliberately keep each contract's weekly/fortnightly visit on its zone's day, so new sign-ups slot into an existing route rather than creating a new drive.
- **Target 5 maintenance jobs per cleaner-day** at ~45 min each + travel, within one cluster. That density is the ~60% gross-margin assumption.
- **Premium the pre-open slot.** Recovery studios, bathhouses and City Cave-style venues want the sauna cleaned **before opening (5-8am)**. Price a slot premium for that window (and it may carry award early-morning penalties you must recover). Book these first each morning, then roll into daytime hotel/apartment/gym sites.
- **Batch quarterlies geographically too.** When a zone has its deep-clean quarter, cluster the 2-4 hr restorations across a couple of days in that zone rather than scattering them.
- **Only expand a new zone (GC/SC) once density justifies it.** Trigger = >15 BNE sites (per the 90-day plan). Do not open Gold Coast with 3 scattered sites; you will destroy the route economics.
- **Sell density into the sales motion.** When you sign a venue, immediately prospect the 2-3 nearest target venues so the cleaner's day fills the same pocket. Multi-site franchisees (City Cave) are a density gift: one rate card, clustered locations.

### 5d. The lever (call it out explicitly)

- **CAC and route density decide the outcome, not price or technique.** The gap between the $72k Conservative and $270k Stretch case is almost entirely (a) how cheaply you acquire the next contract and (b) how tightly it clusters into an existing cleaner-day.
- **Low CAC channels are already yours:** SPARR gym pipeline (every partner convo carries a sauna intro), Blake/SolarShield lead systems + RepFlow review flywheel, free-first-deep-clean-for-testimonial direct outreach, and owning "sauna cleaning Brisbane" (near-zero SEO competition). Model CAC near-zero on network channels and only pay Google Ads for incremental fill.
- **Density multiplier:** each additional site in an existing suburb/day raises cleaner-day revenue toward the $800 ceiling at almost no added travel cost, so its marginal margin is higher than the 60% blended figure. Each site in a NEW scattered zone does the opposite. Sales priority = nearest-neighbour venues first.
- **Honest ceiling:** BNE commercial sauna count is low hundreds; single-city ceiling ~$200-400k rev. Break past it only via (a) GC/SC expansion (trigger >15 BNE sites), (b) licensing the SOP nationally, or (c) bolt-on adjacent technical-cleaning verticals. This is a cash-flow asset, not a venture asset.
