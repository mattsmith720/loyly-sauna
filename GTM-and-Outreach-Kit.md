## 2. The "Free First Deep Clean" Offer Mechanics

**The offer (Tier 1 only):** one free quarterly-grade deep clean and restoration on one sauna. No invoice, no obligation. In exchange for two things: a short written testimonial if they are happy, and the right to use the before/after photos.

**Why it works.** A generic cleaner sells time. We sell proof and risk removal. The free deep clean is not a discount, it is a demonstration of the asset-protection service they cannot see until they see it.

**The mechanics, step by step:**

1. **Pre-service condition report.** Before touching anything, photograph every bench, wall, corner, heater and the underside of benches. UV-torch the mould-prone spots. This doubles as the signed damage-liability record and the "before" set.
2. **Do the full restoration.** Dustless orbital sand of the contact benches back to raw cedar, wood-safe antimicrobial treatment, sweat-salt removal, heater-stone inspection, fastener tighten, ventilation/drainage check.
3. **The after set.** Same angles, same light. Raw sanded cedar next to grey salt-stained timber is the entire sales pitch. It sells itself.
4. **The report converts the contract.** Deliver a one-page photo report: before/after grid, three findings ("bench undersides had early mould we treated," "two fasteners were loose from heat," "at this wear rate benches need sanding every 4-5 months to defer a $20-60k refurb"). Then one line: "To keep it at this standard we run a weekly maintenance visit plus a quarterly deep clean. For your one sauna and plunge that is around $X/month, photo report after every visit."
5. **Anchor the number.** Always frame the monthly fee against the refurbishment they just avoided and the Google-review risk of a visibly grubby sauna. Never against an hourly cleaning rate.

**Cap it.** Two free deep cleans in the first 30 days, reserved for the two best logos (Vikasati, a Bathhouse Albion or TotalFusion). Everyone else gets the photo report from those two as social proof and goes straight to a paid proposal.

## 6. Weekly Outreach Cadence (under 2 hours)

Run it in two blocks. The list from section 1 is your queue; work top-down, Tier 1 first.

**Block A - Monday, ~60 min (send)**
- 15 min: send 5 fresh cold emails/DMs from the Tier list (rotate email, IG, hotel, strata so nothing looks mass-sent). Personalise the one-line detail each time.
- 15 min: run the follow-up sequence. Anyone at touch 1-4 who has not replied gets their next touch. Stop on reply.
- 15 min: post one before/after to GBP and one to Instagram/reels (reuse Matt's SPARR organic-content playbook; sanding clips are the hook).
- 15 min: ask every job completed last week for a Google review via RepFlow; check GBP messages and Ads search-terms report, add any junk terms to negatives.

**Block B - Thursday, ~45 min (walk-ins + warm)**
- 30 min: while already in a suburb cluster for a job, walk into 2-3 nearby venues with the flyer and the walk-in script. Route density is the whole game, so only door-knock where you already are.
- 15 min: send 1-2 warm intros through the SPARR pipeline or Blake/SolarShield (section 4). One good warm intro beats ten cold emails.

**Weekly targets:** 5 cold touches + all due follow-ups + 2-3 walk-ins + 1-2 warm intros + 1 content post + review requests sent. That is the entire funnel in under 2 hours, and it is the CAC-and-route-density lever that decides whether LOYLY lands at $80k or $180k ARR.
